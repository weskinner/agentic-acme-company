# 01. LLC Formation Documents (Core)

## Purpose

This document outlines the legal paperwork typically required to form and operate an LLC in the US and to keep it in good standing.

## Important Note

This is not legal or tax advice. State rules differ. Confirm requirements with your Secretary of State website, CPA, and attorney.

## Core Document Set

## 1) Articles of Organization (or Certificate of Formation)
- Purpose: Creates the LLC as a legal entity.
- Usually includes:
  - LLC legal name
  - Principal business address
  - Registered agent name and address
  - Management structure (member-managed or manager-managed)
  - Organizer information
- Filing authority: Secretary of State (or equivalent state agency).
- Deliverables:
  - Filed stamped copy
  - Filing receipt/confirmation number

## 2) State Filing Receipt/Certificate
- Purpose: Proof that the entity was accepted by the state.
- Keep copies for:
  - Banking
  - Payment processors
  - Vendor onboarding
  - Insurance applications

## 3) Operating Agreement
- Purpose: Internal governance rules for ownership, management, voting, and economics.
- Key terms to define:
  - Ownership percentages and capital accounts
  - Profit/loss allocations
  - Voting thresholds (ordinary vs major decisions)
  - Manager authority and limits
  - Member admission/removal procedures
  - Transfer restrictions and buyout mechanics
  - Dissolution process
- Even single-member LLCs should have one for legal and banking credibility.

## 4) EIN Confirmation (IRS)
- Purpose: Federal tax ID for banking, payroll, and tax filings.
- Keep the IRS confirmation notice with formation records.

## 5) Initial Written Consents/Resolutions
- Purpose: Formally authorize initial company actions.
- Typical approvals:
  - Adopt operating agreement
  - Open bank account
  - Approve accounting method/fiscal year
  - Authorize key contracts

## 6) Registered Agent Designation
- Purpose: Ensures legal notices can be served.
- Maintain current information to avoid missed deadlines/judgments.

## 7) Business Licenses/Permits
- Common areas to check:
  - City/county business license
  - State sales tax permit
  - Home occupation permit (if home-based)
  - Trade name/DBA filing

## 8) Annual Report and Compliance Calendar
- Track recurring obligations:
  - Annual report due date
  - State franchise/business privilege taxes
  - Registered agent renewal
  - Local license renewals

## Formation Data Intake Checklist
- Preferred LLC name + backup names
- Formation state
- Business purpose description
- Registered agent info
- Member/manager legal names and addresses
- Management type (member vs manager)
- Effective date (immediate or delayed)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Filing entity before checking trademark conflicts.
- Missing annual report deadlines.
- Not separating personal and business funds.
- Using generic operating agreement language that does not match actual governance.

## Internal File Organization Suggestion
- Keep in a folder: final signed PDFs + filing receipts + editable drafts.
- Name files consistently: `YYYY-MM-DD_document-name_v1`.
