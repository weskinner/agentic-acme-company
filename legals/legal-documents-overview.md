# Legal Documents Overview

## Important Note

This is a practical planning checklist, not legal advice. LLC and securities rules vary by state and country. Use this as a working draft and have a qualified business attorney review your final documents.

## 1. LLC Formation Documents (Core)

Detailed file: [01-llc-formation-documents.md](01-llc-formation-documents.md)

These are typically needed to form and operate the company.

- Articles of Organization (or Certificate of Formation)
- State filing receipt/confirmation
- Operating Agreement (single-member or multi-member)
- EIN confirmation letter from the IRS
- Initial resolutions or written consent of members/managers
- Registered agent designation
- Business license applications (state/county/city as required)
- Annual report/compliance calendar

## 2. Ownership and Partner Onboarding Documents

Detailed file: [02-ownership-partner-onboarding.md](02-ownership-partner-onboarding.md)

If you are bringing in a partner, these are usually the key documents.

- Amended and Restated Operating Agreement
- Membership Interest Purchase Agreement (if partner is buying in)
- Unit/interest grant documents (if equity is granted instead of purchased)
- Capital contribution agreement/schedule
- Vesting schedule and repurchase rights (if ownership vests over time)
- Buy-sell terms (death, disability, deadlock, exit terms)
- Voting/management rights schedule
- IP contribution/assignment agreement (if partner contributes brand/design IP)
- Member written consent approving new partner and terms

## 3. NDA and Confidentiality Documents

Detailed file: [03-nda-confidentiality.md](03-nda-confidentiality.md)

Use NDA documents before sharing sensitive business, design, or vendor information.

- Mutual NDA (for potential partners/collaborators where both sides share confidential info)
- One-way NDA (for contractors, freelancers, manufacturers, agencies)
- Employee/contractor confidentiality agreement
- Data room access terms (if sharing sensitive files during due diligence)

### NDA Clauses to Confirm
- Definition of confidential information
- Permitted use and non-use restrictions
- Term and survival period
- Exclusions (public info, independently developed info, required disclosures)
- Remedies and injunctive relief
- Governing law and venue

## 4. IP and Brand Protection Documents

Detailed file: [04-ip-brand-protection.md](04-ip-brand-protection.md)

For apparel and print-on-demand businesses, IP protection is especially important.

- Trademark search documentation (brand name/logo/tagline)
- Trademark applications and filing receipts
- Copyright ownership records for original artwork
- Work-for-hire and IP assignment agreements (designers/contractors)
- Brand usage guidelines (internal/external)
- DMCA policy and takedown workflow (if applicable)

## 5. Vendor and Commercial Agreements

Detailed file: [05-vendor-commercial-agreements.md](05-vendor-commercial-agreements.md)

These support day-to-day operations with printing vendors and platforms.

- Print-on-demand vendor service agreement
- Supplier/manufacturer terms review (quality, returns, SLAs, indemnity)
- Fulfillment and shipping policy (customer-facing)
- Website Terms of Service
- Privacy Policy
- Refund/return policy
- Influencer/affiliate agreement templates
- Wholesale terms and retailer agreement template (if selling B2B)

## 6. Finance and Compliance Documents

Detailed file: [06-finance-compliance.md](06-finance-compliance.md)

These keep banking, tax, and bookkeeping properly documented.

- Business bank account opening package
- Sales tax registration and resale certificate (if required)
- Accounting policy notes and chart of accounts setup
- Contractor onboarding documents (W-9, 1099 process in US)
- Record retention policy and compliance checklist

## 7. Recommended Sequence (Practical Order)

Detailed file: [07-recommended-sequence.md](07-recommended-sequence.md)

1. Form LLC and obtain EIN.
2. Finalize Operating Agreement (even before partner enters, include future admission mechanics).
3. Register key business licenses and tax accounts.
4. Lock in IP ownership (designer/contractor assignments, trademark search and filing).
5. Put NDA templates in place before partnership and vendor conversations.
6. Execute partner onboarding package (amended Operating Agreement plus equity docs).
7. Finalize vendor/platform terms and customer-facing legal policies.

## 8. Minimum Document Set Before Launch

Detailed file: [08-minimum-document-set-before-launch.md](08-minimum-document-set-before-launch.md)

If you need a minimum viable legal package for launch, prioritize:

- Articles of Organization
- EIN
- Operating Agreement
- NDA template (mutual and one-way)
- IP assignment agreement template
- POD vendor agreement review
- Website Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, refund/return policy
- Partner onboarding docs (if partner joins before launch)

## 9. Action Checklist

Detailed file: [09-action-checklist.md](09-action-checklist.md)

- Choose formation state and file LLC.
- Draft or review Operating Agreement.
- Decide partner economics (equity %, vesting, capital contribution, decision rights).
- Prepare NDA and IP templates for all collaborators.
- Confirm trademark availability for brand and key marks.
- Review vendor terms for liability, quality standards, and dispute process.
- Have counsel review the full packet before signing.
